# Wedding Invitation — Ksenija & Đorđe

## Product purpose
A single-page digital wedding invitation for Ksenija Subotić and Đorđe Novčić, ceremony on 12 September 2026. Guests open it from a phone link (SMS/WhatsApp) and use it for: ceremony schedule, locations, RSVP, calendar save, and a small emotional moment that telegraphs *this is a real wedding worth showing up to*.

## Register
**Brand.** The page IS the product. There is no underlying tool to expose. Every pixel is in service of one feeling: "we want you here." Design quality directly affects the perceived seriousness of the event.

## Users
- Family members and close friends, ages roughly 25–75, mostly in Serbia, opening the link on mobile.
- Skim-readers: they want date, time, where, and a way to confirm. Anything else has to earn its place.
- Some older guests with low patience for animation that feels gimmicky. Motion must read as *craft*, not *demo reel*.

## Tone
- **Sober and warm.** This is an Orthodox church wedding with traditional Serbian elements (skup svatova, građansko venčanje). The page should feel like a letter, not an app.
- **Quietly confident.** No emojis, no exclamation marks, no "Save the Date!!!". Serif typography, restrained motion, deliberate white space.
- **Serbian-first** copy. Latin script. Tech terms can pass through in English where it's natural; user-facing text doesn't.

## Brand
- **Colors:** ivory paper, burgundy wax (oxblood), aged gold foil. Background is deep oxblood-tinted near-black. The palette is a tight triad — no fourth color enters without strong reason.
- **Type:** Cormorant Garamond for display and names (serif, slightly condensed feel), Montserrat for eyebrows / metadata / UI (geometric sans), Great Vibes only if a script flourish is explicitly needed.
- **Photography & illustration:** the existing line-art couple illustration is the only figurative element. No stock photos. No icons of "wedding rings / champagne glasses" — already too cliché.

## Anti-references
- AI-generated wedding stock with rose-gold gradients and floral-corner frames everywhere.
- Etsy template invitations with calligraphy script as the only typographic gesture.
- "RSVP" cards that feel like a Mailchimp signup form.
- The previous canvas frame-based intro (19 raster crossfades) — looked like AI stop-motion, the very thing we're moving away from.

## Strategic principles
1. **The intro earns one moment, not three.** Open the envelope, the letter emerges, the page begins. No extra theatrics.
2. **Mobile portrait first.** Desktop is a fallback; layout commits to vertical.
3. **Performance is brand.** Hero load under 1s on 4G. Animations 60fps on a five-year-old phone. A slow invitation is a tacky invitation.
4. **The envelope reveal is the only "wow."** Everything after it is calm — schedule, RSVP, countdown — and should feel almost utilitarian by comparison.
